<Section icon={Trophy} title="Deep Dive into build a bucket game modes">

To consistently win matches, you must tailor your player build to the specific demands of your chosen court. The meta shifts dramatically depending on how many players are sharing the floor. Let us break down the three primary competitive arenas and rank them based on tactical depth, rewards, and build accessibility.

### 1v1 Street Duel: The Ultimate Iso Test
The 1v1 arena is a ruthless environment. With no teammates to help on defense or bail you out on offense, your player must be entirely self-sufficient. This mode heavily favors builds with high speed with ball, elite ball-handling, and respectable perimeter defense. If your build cannot create its own shot or stop a quick first step, you will find this mode incredibly punishing.

### 3v3 Blacktop: The Sweet Spot of Synergy
This is where the majority of the community spends their time. The 3v3 format provides enough space for creative dribble drives while still emphasizing team play. A standard competitive lineup consists of a "Play-Sharp" (Playmaking Sharpshooter) to run the offense, a "Lock-Sharp" (Lockdown Sharpshooter) to guard the opposing ball-handler, and a "Glass-Cleaner" (Rebounding Rim Protector) to control the paint.

### 5v5 Pro-Am: Full-Court Simulation
Pro-Am is the pinnacle of competitive play. It features full-court gameplay, standard basketball rules, and requires high levels of team coordination. Here, specialized builds are highly valued over generalist profiles. Teams run structured offensive plays, execute pick-and-roll coverages, and frequently switch to zone defenses to shut down perimeter threats.
