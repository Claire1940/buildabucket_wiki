<Callout type="warning" title="Allocation Warning">
The simulation engine heavily penalizes builds with a single "F" grade. It is often better to take a "B" grade in a weak category than to chase an "A+" in a category where you already have an "A".
</Callout>

Each category requires a unique profile. For instance, **Athleticism** supports your defensive coverage and transition play, but it cannot replace a specialized skill like **Passing**. During a run, you should prioritize "scarce" skills—attributes that only a few players in the NBA excel at, such as elite rim protection or 40% three-point shooting.

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Step-by-Step 99 OVR Optimization Guide">

Achieving a 99 OVR in the **build a bucket random player challenge** requires near-perfect allocation. You must match the best possible player to their most dominant real-world trait.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initialize the Run">
    Start your run by identifying which categories are the hardest to fill. In the 2026 meta, elite Passing and Post D are rarer than Finishing or Mid-range.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Identify the Specialist">
    When the wheel lands on a specialist (e.g., Stephen Curry), assign them immediately to their primary category (Threes). Do not waste a specialist on a secondary trait.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Manage Versatile Stars">
    If you get a superstar like Giannis Antetokounmpo, check your empty slots. If Finishing is open, use him there. If Finishing is full but Athleticism is empty, he is an elite backup for that slot.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The 'Weak Link' Rule">
    By the 6th or 7th spin, identify your lowest projected grade. Use your next "good" player to fix that weakness rather than making a strong category even stronger.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Final Slot Survival">
    The last spin is pure luck. Try to leave a "flexible" category like Mid-range or Athleticism for the final spin, as more NBA players have acceptable grades there compared to Post D or Passing.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid Reputation Traps">
Do not select a player just because they are famous. A lower-rated specialist often provides a higher category grade in **build a bucket** than a legendary superstar who is past their prime or playing out of position.
</Callout>
