Executing the draft requires a systematic approach. You cannot simply pick the biggest name on every spin; you must treat the nine-slot board as an empty puzzle where every piece must fit perfectly. Following a structured step-by-step process ensures that you minimize the impact of bad luck and maximize the value of every superstar that appears.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Choose Your Game Mode">
    Decide whether to play the Normal Wheel Run for a randomized challenge or use Sandbox Mode to test player grades. Sandbox Mode is highly recommended for beginners to understand how specific players affect final attribute ratings before attempting a competitive run.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Establish a Balanced Foundation">
    In your first three spins, aim to secure at least one primary scorer, one playmaker, and one defender. This prevents you from getting backed into a corner where you have elite shooting but zero defensive resistance or play creation.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Preserve Versatile Stars">
    If you roll a multi-category superstar early, do not waste them on an easily filled slot. For example, if you spin Nikola Jokic, do not immediately use him for finishing if your Passing or Rebounding slots are still empty. Save him for the playmaking hub.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Execute Late-Run Mitigation">
    When only three slots remain, stop chasing perfect strengths. Focus entirely on raising the grade of your weakest category. A single C-grade attribute will drag your final overall rating down significantly more than an A+ will raise it.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Run the Season Simulation">
    Submit your finished athlete to test their performance. The simulator will run a full season, tracking team wins, individual statistics, and postseason award outcomes to show how well your drafted attributes synergized.
  </Step>
</Steps>
