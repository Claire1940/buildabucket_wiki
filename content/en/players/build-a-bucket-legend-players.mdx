<Section icon={Trophy} title="Step-by-Step 99 OVR Build Strategy">

Achieving a 99 OVR requires a disciplined approach to the wheel. You cannot simply pick the most famous player; you must pick the player that provides the largest numerical upgrade to your weakest remaining slot.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Secure a Top-3 Specialist">
    Within the first three spins, aim to lock in an **S-Tier specialist**. This is usually a Passing (Jokic/Haliburton) or Post D (Wembanyama) pick. Having an A+ in a core category early sets the floor for your OVR.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Identify the Scarcity Gap">
    By spin five, look at your remaining slots. If you lack **Perimeter Defense or Threes**, prioritize these over "easy" slots like Mid-range or Athleticism, which many players can fill decently.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="The Multi-Category Pivot">
    If the wheel gives you a versatile star like **Giannis Antetokounmpo**, don't rush. Use him to "repair" the category that looks the weakest (either Finishing or Athleticism) to keep all grades above a B.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Final Slot Management">
    Never leave **Passing or Post D** for the final spin. The probability of getting a specialist in the last round is low. Try to leave **Mid-range or Rebounding** for the end, as the variance in these grades is less punishing.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Rating Calculation">
The OVR is not a simple average. **Defensive and Playmaking** grades carry significant weight in the final calculation. A build with all B+ offensive stats and A+ defensive stats will often outrate a pure offensive build.
</Callout>

</Section>
